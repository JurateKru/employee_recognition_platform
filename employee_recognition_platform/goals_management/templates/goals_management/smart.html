📌Tips:

1. 🎯: Specific

In order for a goal to be effective, it needs to be specific.
A specific goal answers questions like:

  • What needs to be accomplished?
  • Who’s responsible for it?
  • What steps need to be taken to achieve it?

    Thinking through these questions helps get to the heart of what you’re aiming for.

    2. 📈: Measurable

    Specificity is a solid start, but quantifying your goals (that is, making sure they’re measurable)
    makes it easier to track progress and know when you’ve reached the finish line.

    3. 🏋️: Achievable

    This is the point in the process when you give yourself a serious reality check.
    Goals should be realistic — not pedestals from which you inevitably tumble.
    Ask yourself: is your objective something your team can reasonably accomplish?

    4. 🌟: Relevant

    Here’s where you need to think about the big picture.
    Why are you setting the goal that you’re setting?

    5. 🗓️: Time-bound

    To properly measure success, you and your team need to be on the same page about when a goal has been reached.

  • What’s your time horizon?
  • When will the team start creating and implementing the tasks they’ve identified?
  • When will they finish?

    • SMART goals should have time-related parameters built in,
    so everybody knows how to stay on track within a designated time frame.
